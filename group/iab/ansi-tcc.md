# ANSI Technology Consortium Council (TCC)

## Overview
The ANSI Technology Consortium Council (TCC) is a forum established by the American National Standards Institute (ANSI) to facilitate collaboration between technology consortia and traditional standards development organizations (SDOs). 

- [Official page](https://www.ansi.org/standards-coordination/consortia/technology-consortium-council)  
- [Announcement](https://www.ansi.org/standards-news/all-news/7-3-25-ansi-forms-new-technology-consortium-council-for-collaboration-with-consortia-community)  


### Charter
From https://www.ansi.org/standards-coordination/consortia/technology-consortium-council
>The purpose of the Technology Consortium Council (TCC) is to:
>1. Provide a forum for discussion and networking among senior managers of leading Consortia;
>2. Provide a mechanism for Consortia representatives to meet and exchange views with ANSI and SDO representatives regarding timely matters of common concern;
>3. Identify issues of common interest regarding evolving U.S., EU, China and other governmental policies that may impact technical standards developers and/or their members; and
>4. Identify possible opportunities for action.


### Context
- The standards landscape has become more complex and policy-influenced (e.g., export controls, geopolitics). 
- Many technology standards are developed in **consortia outside traditional SDOs**, creating coordination gaps. 
- TCC is intended to bridge these communities and improve information sharing and alignment. 

### Scope and Activities
- Regular (primarily virtual) meetings among invited participants  
- Discussion of:
  - Standards development practices  
  - Participation challenges  
  - Government policy impacts 
  - Emerging technology coordination  

### Membership
- By invitation only  
- Participants include:
  - Non-profit consortia  
  - Standards alliances and forums  
  - Organizations developing or promoting standards or best practices
