# Independent New Participant Initiatives

## Overview

This page documents **independently organized, non-IETF programs** that support new participants in engaging with IETF meetings and activities.

These initiatives are run by external entities (e.g., regional groups, foundations) and often target specific communities, geographies, or cohorts.

This page is a **neutral reference** to capture examples and shared practices.

## Scope

- These initiatives are **not sponsored, endorsed, or operated by the IETF**.
- They are **distinct from the official IETF New Participant Program (NPP)**.
- The purpose is **documentation and knowledge sharing**.

## Typical Characteristics

Most initiatives include some combination of:

- **Targeted cohorts** (e.g., students, regional participants)
- **Pre-meeting preparation** (IETF basics, how to engage)
- **Coordinated meeting participation** (selected sessions, guidance)
- **Post-meeting follow-up** (continued engagement)

Most of them provide **financial or logistical support**.

## Why They Exist

These programs typically address:

- Regional participation gaps  
- Community-specific outreach needs  
- Language or cultural barriers  
- Local ecosystem engagement  

They complement general onboarding by providing **targeted support**.

## Best Practices

Based on past initiatives, the following practices have been useful:

- Clear positioning: Explicitly communicate independence from IETF

- Defined objectives: Clarify whether the goal is exposure, onboarding, or sustained contribution

- Preparation emphasis: Invest in pre-meeting readiness 

- Cohort building: Encourage peer interaction within the group

- Continuity: Provide mechanisms for post-meeting engagement - MOST IMPORTANT!
