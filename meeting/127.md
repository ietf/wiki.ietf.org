## Airport Transportation :airplane:

There are three major airports near the San Francisco Bay Area: There are three major airports near the San Francisco Bay Area:
•	San Francisco International Airport (SFO)
•	Oakland International Airport (OAK)
•	San Jose Mineta International Airport (SJC)

Note: It is recommended to save the exact name and address of your hotel or destination on your phone when taking a taxi or rideshare, to avoid confusion.

San Francisco International Airport (SFO) – Main International Airport
Most international flights arrive at and depart from SFO. It offers convenient transportation options including Uber/Lyft, taxis, airport shuttles, rental cars, and BART (Bay Area Rapid Transit) directly to downtown San Francisco.

Oakland International Airport (OAK) – Budget & Domestic Flights
Oakland International Airport (OAK) is a popular alternative to SFO, often used by domestic and low-cost airlines.
Transportation options from OAK:
•	Uber / Lyft and taxis (30–45 min to downtown SF, traffic dependent)
•	BART connection via the Oakland Airport Connector → easy access to San Francisco and East Bay
•	Rental cars and airport shuttles
OAK can sometimes be cheaper and less crowded than SFO.

San Jose Mineta International Airport (SJC) – Closest to Silicon Valley 
San Jose Mineta International Airport (SJC) is the most convenient airport for Silicon Valley, South Bay, and nearby cities.
Transportation options from SJC:
•	Uber / Lyft and taxis (45–70 min to San Francisco)
•	Caltrain connection via shuttle → direct train to San Francisco
•	Rental cars and airport shuttles
SJC is smaller, easy to navigate, and ideal if your destination is in the South Bay.

### Train :train:

BART & Metro 
San Francisco and the Bay Area are served by the BART (Bay Area Rapid Transit) system, which connects airports, downtown San Francisco, Oakland, and other cities in the Bay. BART is cost-effective, fast, and convenient. You can purchase tickets at the station machines using cash, credit cards, or a Clipper Card (reloadable transit card). Station staff can assist if needed.

Bay Area Rapid Transit (BART) is the fastest and most convenient way to travel between the airport, downtown San Francisco, and many cities around the Bay Area.
BART website: https://www.bart.gov

Caltrain & Regional Trains 
Caltrain connects San Francisco to the South Bay, including San Jose and Palo Alto. Tickets can be purchased at stations or via the Caltrain mobile app. For longer trips, Amtrak provides regional train services across California and the West Coast.
Caltrain website: https://www.caltrain.com
Amtrak website: https://www.amtrak.com
